Geneva Declaration on the Future of the World Intellectual Property Organization

 

Humanity faces a global crisis in the governance of knowledge, technology and culture.  The crisis is manifest in many ways.

 

²  Without access to essential medicines, millions suffer and die;

ü  Morally repugnant inequality of access to education, knowledge and technology undermines development and social cohesion;

  Anticompetitive practices in the knowledge economy impose enormous costs on consumers and retard innovation;

n  Authors, artists and inventors face mounting barriers to follow-on innovation;

Ø  Concentrated ownership and control of knowledge, technology, biological resources and culture harm development, diversity and democratic institutions;

u  Technological measures designed to enforce intellectual property rights in digital environments threaten core exceptions in copyright laws for disabled persons, libraries, educators, authors and consumers, and undermine privacy and freedom;

u  Key mechanisms to compensate and support creative individuals and communities are unfair to both creative persons and consumers;

u  Private interests misappropriate social and public goods, and lock up the public domain.
